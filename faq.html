Frequently Asked Questions

What is quantum computing and how does it differ from classical computing?

Quantum computing leverages quantum-mechanical phenomena such as superposition and entanglement to perform operations on data. Unlike classical computers that use bits (0 or 1), quantum computers use qubits which can exist in multiple states simultaneously, allowing for exponential processing power for certain types of problems.

What kind of problems can quantum computing solve better than classical computing?

Quantum computers excel at solving complex optimization problems, cryptography, material science simulations, and certain types of machine learning tasks that would take classical computers impractical amounts of time to solve.

How does quantum computing enhance AI capabilities?

Quantum computing can significantly speed up machine learning training processes, optimize neural network architectures, and enable more complex AI models that would be computationally impractical on classical hardware.
